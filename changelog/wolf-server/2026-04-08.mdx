## What's new

- **Bulk actions**: Activate, pause, or clear many patrol assignments, routes, or schedules in one operation from integrated tools.
- **Exports**: Download large reports as structured files when your role allows, with safeguards so exports stay reliable at scale.
- **Mobile schedules**: Field apps can list, update, and remove schedules with the same rules as the dashboard.
- **Richer context**: Schedules can express more real-world patterns; sites can carry descriptions and service expectations; patrol statistics surface friendlier labels and time ranges.
- **Larger rosters**: Staff directories scale further for big organisations; patrol listings show who is assigned more clearly.
- **Diagnostics**: Engineers can run documented checks when environment or auth issues appear in staging.

## Improvements

- Patrol event ordering is stable when many events share the same minute.
- Schedule execution respects organisation time zones more reliably than server-local time alone.
- Special device tokens are recognised without being mistaken for standard browser sessions.
- Quieter admin logging in production unless explicitly debugging.

## Bug fixes

- Fixed cases where turning a patrol on or off accidentally cleared assignee information.
- Fixed validation and activation edge cases for one-off schedules and route checkpoints.
- Improved handling of linked routes and schedules when data arrives in different shapes from the database.

## Removed

- The **device grant / permission-request** subsystem for patrol configuration is retired in favour of simpler defaults and explicit flags only where still required.
- Unused realtime services for patrol events and reports were removed as dead code.
